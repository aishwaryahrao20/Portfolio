EV Power Module Packaging: From Conventional to Next-Gen Integration

A team research project analyzing next-generation packaging architectures, materials, and thermal topologies for Wide-Bandgap semiconductors in electric vehicle applications.

Overview

This team research project — completed as part of EEE 518 at Arizona State University — examined the thermal, mechanical, and electrical constraints that limit current power module packaging, and evaluated next-generation architectures capable of supporting SiC and GaN devices in 800 V EV systems. The work spanned substrate materials, interconnect technologies, cooling topologies, and real-world commercial benchmarks, culminating in both a detailed technical report and a class presentation.

What we covered

  • Analyzed thermo-mechanical failure modes in conventional wire-bonded, single-sided-cooled power modules — including solder fatigue, CTE mismatch, and wire-bond lift-off.
  • Evaluated advanced ceramic substrates (AlN, Si₃N₄ AMB) and emerging organic dielectrics (ERCD / eIMS) for thermal performance, reliability, and cost tradeoffs.
  • Assessed Double-Sided Cooling (DSC) and baseplate-free architectures against thermal resistance and power-cycling lifetime metrics.
  • Benchmarked commercial implementations from Wolfspeed, ON Semiconductor, Semikron-Danfoss, and BorgWarner.
  • Reviewed wire-bondless interconnect innovations — copper clips, direct lead bonding, and PCB-embedded die — for their impact on parasitic inductance and reliability.
  • Examined silver sintering as a high-temperature die-attach alternative to conventional lead-free solder.

Engineering focus

  • Mapped the full failure chain from fast SiC switching → parasitic stray inductance → voltage overshoot → device stress, showing why packaging is now the limiting factor.
  • Quantified the thermal resistance improvement of DSC modules: ON Semiconductor's VE-Trac Dual demonstrated ~75% improvement in heat dissipation over single-sided designs.
  • Identified Si₃N₄ AMB substrates as the preferred choice for high-reliability SiC traction inverters, given superior fracture toughness and thinner-layer capability.
  • Evaluated ERCD organic substrates as a disruptive cost alternative — ~⅓ the cost of alumina DBC with 2,000+ thermal cycles to failure vs. ~1,600 for alumina.
