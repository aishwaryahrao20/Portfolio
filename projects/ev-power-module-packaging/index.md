# EV Power Module Packaging: From Conventional to Next-Gen Integration

A systematic technical review of how modern power module packaging must evolve to keep pace with Wide-Bandgap semiconductors in electric vehicle applications.

## Overview

This team research project — completed as part of EEE 518 at Arizona State University — examined the thermal, mechanical, and electrical constraints that limit current power module packaging, and evaluated next-generation architectures capable of supporting SiC and GaN devices in 800 V EV systems.

The work spanned substrate materials, interconnect technologies, cooling topologies, and real-world commercial benchmarks, culminating in both a detailed technical report and a presentation delivered to the class.

## What we covered

- Analyzed the thermo-mechanical failure modes in conventional wire-bonded, single-sided-cooled power modules — including solder fatigue, CTE mismatch, and wire-bond lift-off.
- Evaluated advanced ceramic substrates (AlN, Si₃N₄ AMB) and emerging organic dielectrics (ERCD / eIMS) for thermal performance, reliability, and cost tradeoffs.
- Assessed Double-Sided Cooling (DSC) and baseplate-free architectures against thermal resistance and power-cycling lifetime metrics.
- Benchmarked commercial implementations from Wolfspeed, ON Semiconductor, Semikron-Danfoss, and BorgWarner.
- Reviewed wire-bondless interconnect innovations — copper clips, direct lead bonding, and PCB-embedded die — for their impact on parasitic inductance and reliability.
- Examined silver sintering as a high-temperature die-attach alternative to conventional lead-free solder.

## Engineering focus

- Mapped the full failure chain from fast SiC switching → parasitic stray inductance → voltage overshoot → device stress — to show why packaging, not just the semiconductor, is now the limiting factor.
- Quantified the thermal resistance improvement of DSC modules: ON Semiconductor's VE-Trac Dual demonstrated ~75% improvement in heat dissipation over single-sided designs.
- Identified Si₃N₄ AMB substrates as the preferred choice for high-reliability SiC traction inverters, given superior fracture toughness and thinner-layer capability.
- Evaluated ERCD organic substrates as a disruptive cost alternative: ~⅓ the cost of alumina DBC with up to 2,000+ thermal cycles to failure vs. ~1,600 for alumina.

## Key takeaway

The packaging components — not the semiconductor die — are now the primary bottleneck to realizing the full performance potential of WBG devices. Next-generation EV inverters will require coordinated advances in substrates, interconnects, and cooling topologies.
