Integrate-and-Fire Neuron VLSI Design

A full-custom digital neuron implementation verified from schematic through DRC/LVS-clean layout and post-layout timing extraction.

Overview

This project designed and verified a digital integrate-and-fire neuron from gate-level schematic through physical layout. It emphasizes full-custom layout discipline, transistor-level simulation, parasitic extraction, and timing closure.

What I built

  • Designed the IF neuron and D flip-flop layouts in Cadence Virtuoso.
  • Applied transistor-pair matching, signal shielding, and parasitic-aware routing to meet timing and verification constraints.
  • Validated the design through HSPICE transient simulation and StarRC parasitic extraction.

Measured outcomes

  • Achieved DRC-clean and LVS-passing physical layouts.
  • Implemented the neuron in 391 µm2.
  • Reached a 0.5 ns minimum clock period and 5.79 ps worst-case propagation delay after post-layout verification.
Project details
  • Duration: Jan - May 2025
  • Associated with: Arizona State University
  • Project type: VLSI / EDA
  • Area: 391 µm2
  • Verification: HSPICE and StarRC
