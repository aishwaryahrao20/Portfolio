Heterogeneous Integration in Power and Optoelectronics: A Comprehensive Review

A comprehensive survey of heterogeneous integration for SiC/GaN power electronics, co-packaged optics, silicon photonics, and MEMS sensor systems.

View review paper

Overview

This review studies heterogeneous integration across power electronics, data-center photonics, and MEMS sensor systems. It frames integration as a system-level path for combining separately manufactured components into compact, high-performance packages.

Power electronics focus

  • Compared SiC and GaN devices across material properties, voltage domains, device structures, thermal behavior, and robustness.
  • Analyzed packaging challenges such as thermal management, parasitic inductance, reliability, wirebond limitations, and mechanical stress.
  • Surveyed emerging solutions including advanced packaging, GaN-on-diamond substrates, hybrid SiC-GaN modules, co-packaged gate drivers, and 3D integration.

Photonics and sensor focus

  • Reviewed co-packaged optics and silicon photonics for data centers, including bandwidth density, energy efficiency, optical engines, and integration strategies.
  • Covered MEMS integration in automotive, biomedical, industrial IoT, and wearable systems.
  • Compared 2D, 2.5D, 3D, wafer-level, chiplet-based, and system-in-package approaches.

Contribution

  • Built a structured taxonomy of WBG integration techniques and their thermal, electrical, and reliability trade-offs.
  • Created a roadmap view spanning sub-10 pJ/bit photonic interconnects, high-efficiency power conversion, and sub-mm3 smart sensor systems.
  • Consolidated comparative tables and figures for material properties, packaging architectures, and integration approaches.
Project details
  • Duration: Jan - May 2025
  • Associated with: Arizona State University
  • Format: IEEE-style review paper
  • Scope: 2015-2025 literature, 50+ references
  • Domains: WBG power, data-center photonics, MEMS sensors
