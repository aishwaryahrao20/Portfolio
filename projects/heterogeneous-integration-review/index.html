Heterogeneous Integration in Power and Optoelectronics: A Comprehensive Review

An IEEE-format survey covering a decade of progress in heterogeneous integration across WBG power electronics, data-center photonics, and MEMS sensor systems.

Overview

This solo-authored review paper surveys heterogeneous integration — the practice of combining separately manufactured components across disparate technologies into a unified package — from 2015 to 2025. It covers three domains: SiC/GaN power electronics, co-packaged optics (CPO) for data center switches, and MEMS sensor integration in automotive, biomedical, and IoT applications. The goal was to build a unified framework for understanding multi-physics integration challenges and the architectural solutions emerging across industries.

WBG Power Electronics

  • Compared SiC and GaN across material properties, device structures, voltage ratings, and failure modes — correlating each to integration requirements.
  • Analyzed thermal management challenges: GaN-on-Si hotspot formation, SiC high-flux heat removal, and thermal cycling fatigue in power modules.
  • Reviewed parasitic inductance minimization strategies: wirebondless QFN/LGA packages, PCB-embedded die, and co-packaged gate drivers.
  • Surveyed hybrid SiC/GaN module approaches and the roadmap toward power chiplets and system-in-package integration by 2030.

Co-Packaged Optics for Data Centers

  • Compared 2D, 2.5D (interposer/EMIB), and 3D (flip-bonded die) integration techniques for photonic-electronic co-packaging.
  • Reviewed NVIDIA Spectrum-X, Broadcom StrataXGS, and Ayar Labs' TeraPHY as representative CPO implementations.
  • Quantified power efficiency gains: CPO targets ~6–7 pJ/bit vs. 20+ pJ/bit for pluggable modules, with NVIDIA reporting a 3.5× improvement.
  • Analyzed fiber coupling strategies, thermal co-design challenges, and ecosystem barriers to commercial deployment.

MEMS and Sensor Integration

  • Reviewed multi-chip SiP, monolithic MEMS-on-CMOS, and wafer-level heterogeneous bonding — with trade-offs across size, yield, and performance.
  • Covered automotive IMUs, MEMS pressure sensors for EV battery monitoring, and industrial IoT sensor nodes.
  • Surveyed biomedical applications: pacemaker accelerometers, lab-on-chip devices, and implantable pressure sensors.
  • Outlined the roadmap toward smart dust, LiDAR MEMS scanners, and edge AI co-designed with on-package sensors.
Project details
