Driver Distraction & Drowsiness Detection System

A real-time in-vehicle monitoring pipeline that extracts Eye Aspect Ratio, PERCLOS, gaze deviation, and 3-axis head pose from 30 FPS dashcam video — fusing these signals into a closed-loop alert system that triggers in under 3 seconds, validated across 1,600 observations at 86% detection accuracy with no cloud dependency.

Overview

This project built a real-time driver safety monitoring system that continuously analyzes dashcam video to detect drowsiness and distraction, then triggers a closed-loop alert response before the driver's impairment becomes a hazard. The full detection and alerting pipeline runs locally — no cloud inference, no external API — using OpenCV for video processing and dlib's 68-point facial landmark predictor to drive four complementary detection signals: Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR), PERCLOS, gaze ratio, and 3-axis head pose estimation (yaw, pitch, roll).

The system fuses these signals with calibrated thresholds to distinguish genuine drowsiness from transient blinks, gaze from distraction, and normal head movement from disengagement — then actuates a buzzer and dispatches an automated SMS notification when a combined alert condition is met.

Detection signal pipeline

All four detection signals are derived in real time from dlib's 68-point facial landmark mesh applied to each 30 FPS frame. Each signal targets a different physiological indicator of driver impairment.

  • Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR): Computed from the six eye landmark coordinates per eye as the ratio of vertical eye opening to horizontal width. EAR drops sharply during a blink and sustains at a low value during drowsiness-induced eye closure. A threshold of EAR < 0.20 held for ≥ 2.5 seconds is used as the drowsiness trigger, separating involuntary micro-sleeps from normal blink transients.
  • PERCLOS (Percentage of Eye Closure): Tracks the fraction of time per rolling window that the eye is closed beyond the EAR threshold — a clinically established drowsiness metric. High PERCLOS values over a sustained window signal progressive fatigue rather than isolated blink events.
  • Gaze ratio (pupil-to-corner displacement): The horizontal position of the pupil centroid relative to the eye corners is computed per frame to estimate gaze direction. Sustained gaze deviation off the forward-looking axis signals driver distraction independent of eyelid state, catching phone use and mirror-checking behavior that EAR alone cannot detect.
  • 3-axis head pose estimation (yaw, pitch, roll): A set of 3D facial landmark reference points is matched against the 2D detected positions using solvePnP to recover the camera-relative rotation matrix, decomposed into yaw (left/right head turn), pitch (up/down nod), and roll (lateral tilt). Sustained off-axis yaw or pitch flags head-drop and distraction postures that gaze ratio cannot capture when eyes remain forward.

Signal fusion and closed-loop alerting

The four signals are fused under a threshold-based decision logic that requires corroborating evidence before triggering an alert, reducing false positives from individual signal noise. The closed-loop alert sequence is designed to escalate proportionally:

  • Primary trigger — drowsiness: EAR < 0.20 sustained for ≥ 2.5 seconds, confirmed by elevated PERCLOS in the same rolling window, activates the buzzer alert immediately.
  • Secondary trigger — distraction: Sustained gaze ratio deviation (pupil displaced toward eye corner) combined with off-axis yaw or pitch from head pose estimation triggers a separate distraction alert path.
  • Buzzer actuation: Hardware buzzer actuates on either trigger path, providing an immediate physical alert to the driver.
  • Automated SMS notification: An SMS is dispatched automatically via the alert pipeline, notifying a secondary contact — enabling remote awareness for fleet or passenger safety applications.

The entire pipeline — from frame capture to buzzer actuation — completes within a 3-second latency window, measured from drowsiness onset (first frame below EAR threshold) to alert output.

Why four signals?

Driver impairment takes multiple forms that no single signal fully captures. EAR alone misses open-eyed microsleep and distraction. Head pose alone does not distinguish a deliberate head turn from a drowsy nod. Gaze ratio alone cannot detect closed eyes. PERCLOS adds temporal depth to instantaneous EAR, distinguishing a blink from sustained closure. Using all four signals in a fused decision layer provides robust coverage across drowsiness, distraction, and combined impairment states — the three dominant categories of driver inattention in real-world conditions.

Measured outcomes

  • Achieved 86% detection accuracy across 1,600 observations by building a real-time computer vision pipeline that extracted Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR), PERCLOS, gaze ratio (pupil-to-corner displacement), and 3-axis head pose (yaw, pitch, roll) from 30 FPS dashcam video using dlib's 68-point facial landmark detector — validated against labelled ground-truth events across multiple driving sessions.
  • Reduced driver alert response time to under 3 seconds by building a closed-loop control system that fused drowsiness signals (EAR < 0.20 for ≥ 2.5 s) with gaze deviation tracking, triggering buzzer actuation and automated SMS dispatch with no cloud dependency — measured from drowsiness onset frame to physical alert output.
  • Separated drowsiness from transient blinks by combining per-frame EAR thresholding (EAR < 0.20) with rolling-window PERCLOS accumulation, reducing false positive alert rate caused by normal blink events — validated by comparing alert rate against known ground-truth blink vs. drowsiness segments in the test dataset.
  • Detected driver distraction independent of eyelid state by fusing gaze ratio (pupil-to-corner displacement) with 3-axis head pose estimation (solvePnP rotation decomposition), capturing off-axis gaze and head-drop postures that EAR-only systems cannot identify — demonstrated across phone-use and mirror-checking distraction scenarios in the test set.
