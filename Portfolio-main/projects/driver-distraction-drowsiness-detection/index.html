Driver Distraction & Drowsiness Detection System

A real-time in-vehicle monitoring system that turns 30 FPS video signals into distraction, drowsiness, and alert decisions.

Overview

This project built a real-time driver monitoring system that combines facial landmarks, eye metrics, gaze behavior, head pose, and closed-loop alerting to detect distraction and drowsiness from dashcam video.

What I built

  • Built a computer vision pipeline that extracts Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR), PERCLOS, gaze ratio, and 3-axis head pose estimation from 30 FPS dashcam video.
  • Used facial landmark detection to derive yaw, pitch, roll, pupil-to-corner displacement, and drowsiness indicators.
  • Fused drowsiness and gaze-deviation signals into a closed-loop control workflow that triggers buzzer actuation and automated SMS notifications.

Measured outcomes

  • Achieved 86% detection accuracy across 1,600 observations.
  • Reduced driver alert response time to under 3 seconds.
  • Ran without cloud dependency by keeping detection and alert logic local.
Project details
  • Duration: Jan - Aug 2024
  • Associated with: RV Institute of Technology and Management
  • Input: 30 FPS dashcam video
  • Accuracy: 86% across 1,600 observations
  • Alert latency: Under 3 seconds
